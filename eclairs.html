Eclairs

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream (for filling)
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar (for filling)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for filling)
  • 4 oz semisweet chocolate (for topping)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the water and butter to a boil. Once the butter melts, remove from heat.
  3. Stir in the flour and salt until the dough comes together. Return the saucepan to medium heat for 2-3 minutes to dry out the dough, stirring constantly.
  4. Transfer the dough to a bowl and allow it to cool slightly for 2 minutes. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, making sure each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. The dough should be smooth and glossy.
  5. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a large round tip. Pipe long strips of dough, about 4 inches in length, onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between each one.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the éclairs are golden and puffed up. Turn off the oven and leave the éclairs inside for an additional 10 minutes to dry out.
  7. Allow the éclairs to cool completely on a wire rack.
  8. For the filling, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a bowl until stiff peaks form. Transfer the whipped cream to a piping bag.
  9. For the chocolate topping, melt the semisweet chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl over simmering water or in the microwave in 30-second intervals. Stir until smooth.
  10. Once the éclairs have cooled, cut a small slit along the side of each éclair. Pipe the whipped cream filling into the center of each éclair.
  11. Dip the top of each éclair into the melted chocolate mixture and allow the excess to drip off. Let the chocolate set before serving.
